# CMIT 321 — Ethical Hacking

Coursework folder for **UMGC CMIT 321: Ethical Hacking**. This is a working space for the two graded projects and two project-related discussions defined in the course "Projects Overview."

> **Scenario:** You have been hired as a Security Operations Center (SOC) Analyst — Blue Teamer at *Centralia Technology*, a cybersecurity services company helping organizations secure their digital assets. Your manager is putting together teams for an upcoming Capture the Flag (CTF) competition. The work in this folder simulates that program: individual CTF practice, team collaboration, and a final class-wide presentation.

## What lives here

| Folder | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| `project-1-ctf-writeup/` | Project 1 — Capture the Flag Write-Up (Week 3, individual) |
| `project-2-ctf-presentation/` | Project 2 — CTF Solution Presentation (Week 7, resubmission Week 8) and the two project-related discussions |
| `resources/` | Field guide notes, references, and tooling docs that support both projects |

## Project list

1. **Project 1 — Capture the Flag Write-Up** *(Week 3, individual)*
   Identify one or two CTF challenges you didn't attempt or couldn't complete, document the techniques, tools, websites, or other resources that can help solve them, answer 10 questions from the in-house CTF quiz bank, and submit the company survey.

   2. **Project 2 — Capture the Flag Solution Presentation** *(Week 7, resubmission Week 8)*
      Present your CTFs to your team and give feedback to teammates. Two project-related discussions support this:
         - **Collaborative CTF Discussion** (Weeks 4–7) — collaborate with assigned team members to solve more CTF challenges.
            - **CTF Class Presentation / Discussion** (Week 8) — present your CTFs to the class and give feedback to classmates.

            ## Ground rules for this folder

            - No CTF flag values, quiz answer keys, or copyrighted UMGC course material are committed here.
            - Screenshots that include credentials, IPs, or hostnames are redacted before commit.
            - Each write-up emphasizes **method, tooling, and lessons learned** over solutions.
